The Platform

Not Edutainment.
A Learning Engine.

FaeryQuest turns any curriculum into an action-adventure RPG. Progress is mastery-gated: boss fights and puzzles launch quiz questions drawn directly from course material. Answer correctly to deal damage; miss and you take it.

An automated content pipeline ingests textbooks, aligns them to standards, and outputs structured JSON — lessons, quizzes, metadata — ready to play. Educators go from raw materials to a fully playable game without writing a single line of code.

The platform also supports natural-language course creation: describe what you want to teach and the pipeline builds it for you. Accessible to any educator, at any technical level.

Market Opportunity

Massive Budgets.
Proven Willingness to Pay.

The U.S. PreK–12 market generates $5.3 billion in annual instructional materials revenue. State budgets guarantee per-student spend — Texas alone allocates $212 per student for approved instructional materials. The money is already earmarked.

Outside the classroom, 1.8 million homeschool families routinely spend $100–$215 per course. Our $49 price point sits well below the category norm while still providing healthy margins.

Corporate training adds another $98 billion in addressable spend — an adjacent market once the core product is proven. The opportunity is real, budgeted, and recurring.

Business Model

Two Revenue Lanes.
Both with Clear Unit Economics.

Consumer Lane · B2C
$49 / course

How the math works (clear)

Target: U.S. homeschool market (~1.765M learners). A 1% penetration equals 17,650 course sales in year 1.

Revenue at $49/course: $864K gross. Early platform incentives (Epic Games Store) can reduce fees on initial revenue, improving early net receipts before marketing and QA costs.

Even conservative uptake (0.2%) yields meaningful validation revenue (~$120K), enough to fund continued development and pilot programs.

Note: platform revenue shares and distribution costs vary; figures assume initial promotional fee waivers where available (source).

Institutional Lane · B2B
$15 / seat / yr

Pilot & Scale

Example pilot: 10,000 students at $15/seat/year = $150K ARR. Pilots validate implementation, reporting, teacher dashboards, and standards alignment.

School budgets (e.g., Texas instructional materials allotments) often cover such costs, removing procurement barriers in many districts.

Platform and privacy: COPPA & FERPA compliant; supports offline modes and SSO for district rollouts.
